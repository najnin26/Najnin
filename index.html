Journal Article Intelligence-Based Medicine — Elsevier 2025
Quantum Computing in Medical Diagnostics and Treatment: A Systematic Review of Trends, Challenges and Future Directions
Najnin Sultana Shirin, Md Mehedi Hasan, Md Kishor Morol, Nafiz Fahad, Md Tanzib Hossain, Md Jakir Hossen, Dip Nandi
This systematic review comprehensively surveys the intersection of quantum computing and medical diagnostics, covering quantum algorithms for imaging analysis, drug discovery, genomic data processing, and treatment optimization. We identify key trends including variational quantum eigensolvers, quantum kernel methods for disease classification, and hybrid quantum-classical pipelines for clinical decision support. We map challenges across hardware noise, qubit coherence, and scalability, and outline future directions for NISQ-era medical AI.

Journal Article IEEE Transactions on Quantum Engineering 2025
Q-EmotionNet: A Quantum Convolutional Neural Network for Facial Emotion Recognition via Variational Quantum Circuits
Najnin Sultana Shirin, Md Kishor Morol, Tze Hui Liew, Dip Nandi
We propose Q-EmotionNet, a quantum convolutional neural network that encodes facial feature maps into parameterized quantum states and processes them through variational quantum circuits. Our model achieves competitive emotion classification accuracy while demonstrating favorable parameter efficiency compared to classical CNNs. Noise resilience experiments on IBMQ simulators and real hardware analyze circuit depth and entanglement topology effects on performance.

Journal Article Quantum Machine Intelligence — Springer Nature 2025
Hybrid Quantum–Classical Transfer Learning Using Vision Transformer Features for Breast Cancer Histopathology
Najnin Sultana Shirin, Md Kishor Morol, Tze Hui Liew, Dip Nandi
We present a hybrid quantum-classical transfer learning framework for breast cancer histopathology classification. Visual features extracted via a pre-trained Vision Transformer (ViT) are encoded into quantum states and classified through variational quantum circuits. Benchmarked on the BreakHis dataset across multiple magnification levels, the quantum-enhanced pipeline achieves comparable or superior accuracy with fewer trainable parameters.

Journal Article IEEE Open Access 2025
QBERT-LSTM: Quantum Intelligence Based Real-Time Mental Health Detection Using Web Scraping
Najnin Sultana Shirin, Md. Aminul Islam, Maria Akter Abin, Mahmoud Abouyoussef
QBERT-LSTM is a novel quantum-classical hybrid for real-time mental health detection from social media text. Combining quantum-enhanced BERT (QBERT) representations with a classical LSTM model operating on web-scraped data, the framework encodes semantic features into quantum states via variational circuits. Results demonstrate improvements in precision and recall over classical BERT-LSTM baselines, particularly for minority-class detection in imbalanced clinical text datasets.

Conference Paper ICQT'25 2025
ACAM-MobileNetV2: Attention and Contrastive-Aware MobileNetV2 for Multi-Class Viral Skin Lesion Classification
Najnin Sultana Shirin, Shabikun Nahar
We introduce ACAM-MobileNetV2, integrating channel-wise and spatial attention modules with a supervised contrastive loss into MobileNetV2 for multi-class viral skin lesion classification. Evaluated on a curated dermatological dataset, ACAM-MobileNetV2 outperforms vanilla MobileNetV2 and attention-augmented baselines in macro-F1 and per-class accuracy, offering a lightweight diagnostic tool for clinical dermatological AI.
